Meet the Crew

We are a group of aviation workers campaigning for long-term employment. We do this by challenging industry leaders to conform with climate science and reject dangerous growth. As pilots, cabin crew, airport staff, aerospace engineers, factory workers and more, we love the aviation industry and the positive impact it brings to society. Join us now to protect the future of flying, and the planet we love.

Safe Landing is divided into two groups: The Core Team, who work full-time and part-time for us – and the Safe Landing Community: Made up of aviation workers, and non-aviation workers too, the community is full of people who love aviation and care about the future of the planet – everyone is welcome on our journey towards a Safe Landing.

Safe Landing Community

Below are just some of the many people who make up the Safe Landing Community. Using their expertise and knowledge of the aviation industry, the community is vital to the workings of Safe Landing, and our ability to enact change. Without active members we would not achieve anything.

If you would like to use your aviation expertise to fight for change, sign up now or contact us directly to discuss how you’d like to get involved.

Siân Andrews

ATC & Safe Landing Ambassador

Siân originally trained as an En-route Air Traffic Controller, then spent a few years working in Flight Data Monitoring, before going back to Air Traffic Management to work on a large European ATM Research project, where she has over 10 years of experience.

Keiran Tate

Aerospace Engineer & Academic

Kieran is a lifelong environmental activist and PhD researcher in aerospace engineering and atmospheric chemistry, researching the climate impact of aviation and potential short-term mitigation options.

Katie Thompson

After a long flying career as a flight instructor, an Airbus A340 pilot, and then a NetJets Management Pilot, I started looking into the impact that aviation was having on the planet. It was not something that was being discussed around me in any depth. The ultimate result was that I quit my job, stopped working as a pilot and went back to college, among other climate credentials, I now hold a Masters in Sustainable Aviation.

Andy

Pilot

As an airline pilot working for a large UK carrier it’s hard to find opportunities to talk about the climate and ecological impact of our industry. Safe Landing offers me a space to be truthful about my own conflicted and compromised feelings.
